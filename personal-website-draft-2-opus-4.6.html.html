About Me

Engineer first.
Builder always.

I'm Hamza — an AI Product Manager, electrical engineer, and someone who believes the best work happens when you understand a problem deeply enough to simplify it. My career has taken an unconventional path, and I think that's exactly what makes it interesting.

I started with an engineering degree at the University of Manchester, where I learned to think in systems and solve problems from first principles. After graduating, I joined my family's manufacturing business in Riyadh — not as an observer, but as someone on the production floor every single day, timing processes, rearranging factory layouts, and introducing a Kaizen mindset that reduced waste by 45% and inventory by 52% in two years.

That hands-on, data-driven approach followed me into the AI world. At Navid, I led a team of engineers to build Yehia — a 7-billion parameter Arabic language model that achieved the #1 ranking on the OALL leaderboard, outperformed GPT-4o on multiple Arabic benchmarks, and was cited by KAUST in their AralingBench research paper. I didn't just manage the project — I wrote evaluation scripts, designed training pipelines, and presented to government ministries.

Now based in the UK, I'm looking for my next opportunity to build something meaningful at the intersection of AI and product strategy. I bring engineering rigour, a product mindset, and the kind of hustle you only develop when you've scaled a business from the ground up and shipped a language model.
