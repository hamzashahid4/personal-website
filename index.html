About Me

Engineer first.
Builder always.

I'm Hamza — an AI Product Manager, electrical engineer, and someone who believes the best work happens when you understand a problem deeply enough to simplify it. My career has taken an unconventional path, and I think that's exactly what makes it interesting.

I started with an engineering degree at the University of Manchester, where I learned to think in systems and solve problems from first principles. After graduating, I joined my family's manufacturing business in Riyadh — not as an observer, but as someone on the production floor every single day, timing processes, rearranging factory layouts, and introducing a Kaizen mindset that reduced waste by 45% and inventory by 52% in two years.

That hands-on, data-driven approach followed me into the AI world. At Navid, I led a team of engineers to build Yehia — a 7-billion parameter Arabic language model that achieved the #1 ranking on the OALL leaderboard, outperformed GPT-4o on multiple Arabic benchmarks, and was cited by KAUST in their AralingBench research paper. I didn't just manage the project — I wrote evaluation scripts, designed training pipelines, and presented to government ministries.

Now based in the UK, I'm looking for my next opportunity to build something meaningful at the intersection of AI and product strategy. I bring engineering rigour, a product mindset, and the kind of hustle you only develop when you've scaled a business from the ground up and shipped a language model.

University of Manchester

BSc Electrical and Electronic Engineering
September 2013 — September 2016
M

Manchester was the first move away from home — and a life-defining 3 years in a city built for students. Three world-class universities within walking distance, students from all over the world, and I was lucky to be at the best one. The city itself is hard to put into words: vibrant, unpretentious, and the perfect place to figure out who you are.

The Electrical and Electronic Engineering degree was the perfect challenge. I went deep across magnetic fields, power systems, control theory, robotics, machine learning, and software — a broad and rigorous introduction to the discipline that taught me how to think in systems and solve problems from first principles.

Year 2 Group Project

The Embedded Systems Group Project — building an autonomous buggy from scratch

This was my first real team build with people from all over the world. We took an autonomous buggy from a pile of components to a finished racer — picking parts, cutting the chassis, designing circuits in Multisim, soldering capacitors and resistors onto our own boards, then writing the embedded code that drove the sensors and motors.

The whole class divided into teams and we all converged on a single competition: a series of tracks designed with bridges, moving parts, and unexpected obstacles. Every team's buggy was timed, and the fastest progressed through the rounds. Our team made the top 20.

Final Year Dissertation

Advanced Demand Profiling and Management for Smart Grids

Supervised by Prof. Jovica Milanovic · April 2016 · School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering

I built an Artificial Neural Network framework in MATLAB for load disaggregation and demand forecasting in smart grids. The work introduced a probabilistic framework for predicting the Dynamic Response of Demand at bulk supply points and demonstrated the critical role of reactive power (Q) measurements in achieving high-accuracy load estimation.

It was my first real taste of an end-to-end ML pipeline — feature engineering, training, validation, deployment — the same instincts I now apply to enterprise Generative AI.
